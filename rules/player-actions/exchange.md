# Action Card - Exchange

Trading can be done with neighboring empires, empires you have trade agreements with, or with the black market:
    - Trading can be initiated with any empire which is neighboring the players empire. 
    - Or with other empires that have a trading agreement with the player.
    - Or with the black market according to the black market price.

- Trading: 
  1. should be initiated between the active player and any other neighboring player(s) or the other way around
  2. the active player can trade with NP empires such as: 1st Sentients, Ancient Rulers, Galactic trader(s). 
  3. agreements are binding agreements if they involve resources or star systems exchange
  4. promissory agreements needs not to be binding unless this was agreed upon.
  5. there are no limits to what can be traded not even renting of armies, fleets, star systems
  6. a supply and demand indicator shows how much consumer and industrial goods are in general produced in the galaxy and shows the price to buy and sell them to other NP empires

## Trade Agreements

A Trade Agreement allows two empires to trade with each other even when they are not neighboring.

### Establishing a Trade Agreement
- Use the Exchange action to propose a Trade Agreement to another player.
- If both players agree, each player places a **Trade Agreement token** on their tableau indicating the active agreement with the other empire.

### Duration
- Trade Agreements are **open-ended** — they remain active until one player cancels them.

### Cancellation
- Either player may cancel the agreement **at any time** — no action required.
- Both players immediately return their Trade Agreement tokens to the supply.

### Limit — Trade Agreement Slots
- By default, each empire has **1 Trade Agreement slot** — only one active trade agreement at a time.
- Additional slots can be unlocked through tech research.
